# Bench results — 2026-05-24

_Source: 2 run file(s) from host(s) deepu-flowz13-arch, deepu-flowz13-arch-vulkan on backend(s) rocm._

> **Engine A/B: HIP vs Vulkan on AMD Strix Halo (gfx1151).**
> The 2026-05-23 cross-tool run hinted that LM Studio's Vulkan
> backend was faster than our HIP `llama-server` build on small
> workloads. To isolate the engine variable from the tool variable,
> this page re-runs LlamaStash + raw `llama-server` against the same
> byte-identical GGUF (`gemma-4-E2B-it-Q4_K_M.gguf`) on the **same
> upstream llama.cpp commit (b9282)** built two ways:
>
> - HIP build: `/mnt/work/Workspace/llms/llama.cpp/build-hip/bin/llama-server`
> - Vulkan build: `/mnt/work/Workspace/llms/llama.cpp/build-vulkan/bin/llama-server`
>   (RADV STRIX_HALO Vulkan driver)
>
> Pointed via `$LLAMASTASH_LLAMA_SERVER`. The HIP numbers below are
> the 2026-05-23 page's numbers reproduced here side-by-side; the
> Vulkan numbers are the new measurements from this run.
>
> Findings:
>
> | Engine | chat_turn decode | agent_decode |
> | ------ | ---------------- | ------------ |
> | HIP    | ~81 tok/s        | ~81 tok/s    |
> | Vulkan | ~97 tok/s        | ~94 tok/s    |
> | Δ      | **+20%**         | **+17%**     |
>
> Vulkan beats HIP on both workloads on this hardware (AMD Radeon
> 8060S / Strix Halo APU, RDNA 3.5). LlamaStash and raw `llama-server`
> stay within ~1% on either engine, so the wrapper continues to add
> zero measurable overhead.
>
> Caveat: Vulkan's first-spawn shader compilation takes 5+ minutes
> against an empty shader cache. After the cache is warm, subsequent
> spawns are sub-second. The bench harness defaults the readiness
> probe to 180s — raise it via `LLAMASTASH_BENCH_READY_TIMEOUT_S=600`
> for first Vulkan runs.

See [methodology.md](methodology.md) for the matched-pair settings policy, the variance-gate rules, and the conflict-of-interest disclaimer. Charts are deterministic SVG — re-render from the source JSONs to verify.
